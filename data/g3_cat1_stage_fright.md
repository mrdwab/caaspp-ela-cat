## Stage Fright
by Marne Ventura

"I don't want to be the healthy tooth anymore," said Samantha as she walked up the sidewalk to the Multipurpose Room.

"Why not?" asked Samantha's older sister, Megan. "You said it was the best part in the whole play."

"I just don't," said Samantha. The roots on her healthy-tooth costume were flapping against her legs.

"You can't change your mind now," said Samantha's brother, Brian.

"My stomach feels funny," said Samantha.

Mom felt Samantha's forehead.

"You've practiced every night for a month," said Mom. "You'll be great."

"Mrs. Johnson picked you because she knows you can do it," said Dad as he opened the door.

Samantha's teacher waited just inside. "Here's our healthy tooth!" she said, leading Samantha backstage.

"Break a leg," said Megan.

"You'll be wonderful," said Mom.

"We'll be watching," said Dad.

"Watch out for Tooth Decay," said Brian.

Samantha sat down.

Patrick tickled her with the bristles on his toothbrush costume.

"Patrick, are you afraid to go onstage?" asked Samantha.

"My dad said I should pretend I'm just practicing and nobody's watching," answered Patrick.

Anika pretended to squirt Samantha with toothpaste from the top of her toothpaste costume.

"Anika, are you afraid to go onstage?" asked Samantha.

"My big brother told me to pretend that the people in the audience are cabbages," answered Anika.

Maggie was singing the "Tooth Decay" song.

"Maggie, are you afraid to go onstage?" asked Samantha.

"My mom told me to memorize my lines and just have fun," answered Maggie.

Suddenly the backstage lights blinked off and on. The children were quiet.

Mrs. Johnson held out her hand as the lights flooded the stage. "You go out first, Samantha."

Samantha walked slowly to the center of the stage and looked out at all the people. Everyone was quiet.

The lights made her face feel tight and hot.

Samantha couldn't remember what to say.

She closed her eyes and pretended she was at practice and nobody was watching. Her legs wobbled.

She opened her eyes and imagined all the faces staring at her were cabbages. She still couldn't remember her lines.

Her hands sweated.

Samantha thought about all the nights she had practiced her lines.

Dad and Mom and Megan and Brian had helped her.

It had been fun when she had practiced with her family. She had remembered exactly what to say.

Now Samantha knew what to do. She needed to find her family in the audience and pretend she was having fun practicing with them.

Samantha looked for her family. She found them sitting in the second row. She smiled, and they all smiled back.

Samantha took a deep breath. "I am a healthy tooth," she began. Her voice was loud and clear!

"I come in handy every time you smile. . . ."
